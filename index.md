Hi, I'm **Yen-Chia Peng**, an undergraduate researcher in the [Department of Information and Computer Engineering](https://iceweb.cycu.edu.tw/) at [Chung Yuan Christian University](https://www.cycu.edu.tw/). I work in the Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition Lab, advised by Prof. Chih-Chang Yu, where I explore problems at the intersection of **<u>3D vision</u>** and medical imaging—from reconstructing soccer stadiums with 3D Gaussian Splatting to tackling the messy realities of volumetric imbalance in cardiac CT segmentation.

My research interests span computer vision, continual learning, and audio/music information retrieval. Lately, I've been diving deep into **Class-Incremental Semantic Segmentation**, working on a framework that combines self-distillation, attention-guided pseudo-labeling, and CLIP-based semantic alignment to address the notorious *background shift problem*—one of those deceptively tricky challenges that shows up the moment you try to teach a model new things without forgetting the old. This work is currently submitted as an NSTC undergraduate research proposal.

Beyond the lab, I've co-authored papers on social-emotional music classification—collaborating with musicologists and special education experts to build lightweight models that help people with special needs engage with music more meaningfully. One of these projects won the **Best Paper Award** at TANET & NCS 2025, and another was published in [*Machine Learning with Applications* (Elsevier)](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S2666827025002154).

Currently maintaining a **GPA of 3.97/4.0** (ranked 3rd out of 106), though I'd argue the most interesting things I've learned haven't shown up on any transcript yet.

When I'm not in the lab, I enjoy staying on top of the latest AI research and releases—the field moves fast, and honestly, that's half the fun. I also love traveling; exploring new places has a way of sparking ideas that staring at a screen never quite manages.

Feel free to reach out—always happy to chat about new AI techniques, or where to go next! ✈️
